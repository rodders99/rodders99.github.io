## A Bit about me

After leaving school at 16 (1983), I started working in a local furniture store as a shop boy, which I hated. Following on from being, not surprisingly, sacked from a job I hated, I found a job I loved as a shop assistant in my local computer shop.

While this was the early days of IT as we know it today, this small retail shop serviced some of the biggest names in the UK; Ciba Geigy Pharmaceuticals, Rutherford Appleton Laboratory and Bank of America to name a few. We'd regularly take 40ft trailer deliveries of IBM and DEC Computers, which would sit outside the shop while I labelled hundreds of PCs, monitors and keyboards up, for shipping later that day.

To this day I'm still amazed that we never had anything stolen from outside the shop!

### Basingstoke

Following the closure of the shop, I was offerd the opportunity to transfer to the parent company in Basingstoke, then called Micro Peripherals, which in turn was acquired by Sharptext who in turn were acquired by DCC Group and in time, the Micro-P we knew, was rebranded to Exertis.

DCC today are one of the largest investment businesses in the UK specialising in the acquisition and transformation of businesses and operating across four continents with over 16,000 employees worldwide. I consider myself very fortunate to have worked for them and appreciate how much I learnt  working for them.

### Head of this and that

In my roles across the years I was variously Head of Networking, Head of Power Quality, Head of Smart Tech and finally Head of Innovation for Exertis, a large international IT distributor.

As Head of innovation I was given the task to do some "out of the box" thinking and find a new business opportunity.  I had proposed to the business that we get involved in the EV market. After extensive market analysis, budgeting and development work that resulted in commitment from the board to begin this project, I was unfortunately made redundant.

### I enjoy...

I've been in full time employment since I was 16 years old and I've been fortunate that redundancy gave me the opportunity to take a step back and reflect on my 40+ year career to understand what I enjoyed and did not enjoy in previous roles.

The major conclusion I came to is "I Love Data Analytics", the aspects of my previous role that gave me the most satisfaction were identifying the opportunity, researching it, analysing it, building a proposition and getting others on board with the idea.

When not analysing data, learning something new about analysing data, or raising my beloved lillies in the garden, I'm usually fiddling around with my insanely smart home, a hobby project I've been working on for 12 years. To give you some idea of what 12 years of development in a smart homes looks like, there are currently 1,061 smart home entities under my control. sensors, switches, lights, cameras, automations, covering the garden and home.

The early days of my smart home, with a self written voice assistant controlling my home driven by a tablet "hey Google", gave me a clear indication of what the smart home needed to become a mainstream rather than niche technology and it was voice control. It took Amazon, Apple and Google a few years to catch up with me and start producing voice assistants that could control smart home devices!

### New Business

You could say that looking for new opportunities was a theme for me, over thirty years with my previous employer, I started four new business units. The first business unit I started when I was twenty years old and I'll admit, I did not have a a clue what I was doing! But that business unit in Networking is still running today, the next BU was Data Centre Power, following this a new Smart Home business unit was formed and the final business unit was EV charging, and we know how that ended up !

The Smart Home and EV business units coincided more with social media and platforms like LinkedIn which became an outlet for my thought leadership on the smart tech world leading to articles being published by market specific publications like CRN, Retail News and more. I advised market research companies like Context and and GfK as well as working with major retailers such as Currys, AO, Kingfisher group.
